Frequently asked questions

1

What are buyer intent signals?

Buyer intent signals are observable actions or behaviors that show when a prospect is actively evaluating solutions. Quidity tracks 20+ signals, including website activity, company news, product usage, job changes, CRM updates, and third-party insights. Monitoring these in real-time helps your teams prioritize high-intent leads and reach out at the right moment.

2

How do intent signals work?

Intent signals track prospect actions across websites, CRM, product usage, and third-party sources. Quidity filters signals by your ideal customer profile and triggers automated, context-rich outreach to engage prospects when they’re most likely to convert.

3

What is the difference between first-party and third-party intent data?

First-party intent comes from your own properties, like website visits and product usage, offering highly accurate insights. Third-party intent comes from external sources, like G2 reviews or Slack discussions, and helps identify early-stage prospects. Quidity combines both for precise, timely outreach.

4

What are the most important buying signals to track?

Top B2B signals include pricing page visits, demo requests, job changes, content downloads, and competitor review interactions. Multiple signals stacking together indicate high purchase intent, helping teams prioritize outreach for faster conversion.

5

How do you set up custom CRM signals in Quidity?

Custom CRM signals let you trigger actions based on your unique sales logic, like lead score thresholds, opportunity stage changes, or renewal dates. Quidity monitors these conditions and automatically starts personalized outreach sequences or alerts builders.

6

How do I measure ROI from intent signals?

ROI is tracked by measuring response rates, meetings booked, pipeline growth, and revenue from signal-driven leads. Quidity’s analytics show how signals influence engagement and closed deals, helping you optimize sales outcomes quickly.
